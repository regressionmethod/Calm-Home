🛋️ Living Room Calm Setup
📺 Noise & Stimulation Control
  • Turn off background TV when not actively watching
  • Remove or quiet squeaky/noisy toys during calm hours
  • Close blinds/curtains to reduce visual triggers (people, dogs, cars)
💡
Quick Tip: Use white noise or calming music instead of silence. Complete quiet can make dogs more alert to sounds.
🏠 Space & Layout
  • Create clear pathways — remove obstacles that create frustration
  • Designate one specific spot as "dog's space" (mat, bed, or corner)
  • Remove or secure tempting items (shoes, remotes, food)
🌸 Calming Enhancements
  • Add lavender or chamomile scent (dog-safe diffuser or spray)
  • Use softer lighting in evening (lamps vs overhead lights)
⚠️
Avoid: Essential oils like tea tree, eucalyptus, or citrus — these can be overwhelming or toxic to dogs.
🚪 Entry/Door Area Setup
🔔 Doorbell & Arrival Management
  • Move dog's view away from main window/door (use baby gate or furniture)
  • Create a "greeting station" 6+ feet from the door
  • Keep leash and treats easily accessible near the door
💡
Pro Tip: Practice calm door approaches when no one is visiting. Ring the doorbell yourself and reward calm behavior.
👥 Guest Preparation
  • Place a mat or designated spot where dog should wait during greetings
  • Prepare a "guest kit": treats, backup leash, and instruction card
  • Post a small reminder sign for yourself: "Calm energy first"
🍽️ Kitchen/Dining Setup
🥣 Feeding Zone Structure
  • Create a consistent feeding spot away from high-traffic areas
  • Use elevated bowls or a feeding mat to define the space
  • Remove food temptations from dog's reach (counter, floor, trash)
⏰ Routine & Timing
  • Feed at the same times daily to reduce food anxiety
  • Create a pre-meal calm ritual (sit, wait, gentle release cue)
💡
Meal Tip: Use feeding time as a daily reset opportunity. Practice calm commands before meals when motivation is highest.
🛏️ Bedroom/Sleep Setup
🌙 Bedtime Routine
  • Dim lights 30 minutes before desired sleep time
  • Create a consistent "wind down" routine (same order, same timing)
  • Use white noise or calming sounds to mask outside triggers
🛏️ Sleep Environment
  • Provide a comfortable, consistent sleep surface (bed, crate, or mat)
  • Ensure room temperature is comfortable (65-70°F ideal)
⚠️
Sleep Regression Alert: Changes in sleep location or routine often trigger behavioral regression during the day.
🧘 Dedicated Calm Zone Setup
📍 Location & Layout
  • Choose a quiet corner away from main foot traffic
  • Use a specific mat, bed, or blanket to define the space
  • Ensure easy access but also provides some visual privacy
🌿 Sensory Calming Elements
  • Add dog-safe calming scent (lavender spray on blanket or diffuser)
  • Use soft lighting or natural light (avoid harsh overhead lights)
  • Include a comfort item (favorite blanket, soft toy, or your old shirt)
  • Keep calm zone free of exciting toys or stimulating objects
💡
Reset Zone: This is where you'll do your daily 10-minute reset ritual. Make it the most peaceful spot in your home.
